## Part 1 — Discovery Meeting Questions for Belal

Before generating a single lead, you need to understand the business you're representing. Bring these questions to your first working meeting with Belal.

---

### 1A. Understanding the Current Business

1. What types of businesses are your current clients? (Manufacturers? Traders? Importers? Retailers?)
2. What industries do you serve most? (Garments? Electronics? Food? Machinery? Raw materials?)
3. What is your typical shipment size? (Full containers? LCL? Air freight? Mix?)
4. What are your busiest trade corridors today? (Bangladesh → Europe? Malaysia → Middle East? Others?)
5. How many shipments does your company handle per month, roughly?
6. What is the split between ocean freight and air freight in your business?
7. Do you handle both import and export, or mainly one direction?
8. Do you offer warehousing, customs brokerage in-house, or do you subcontract these?

### 1B. Competitive Advantage

9. Why do your current clients choose you over other forwarders? What do they tell you?
10. What do you think you do better than your competitors?
11. Are there specific routes or corridors where you have especially strong rates or service?
12. Do you have exclusive or preferred agent relationships in certain countries that give you an edge?
13. What is your strongest selling point — price, speed, reliability, personal service, or something else?

### 1C. Growth Goals & Gaps

14. Where do you want to grow? More clients in Malaysia? Bangladesh? New countries?
15. Are there industries you want to enter but haven't yet?
16. Are there trade corridors you want to develop but currently lack volume on?
17. What is your capacity right now — can you handle significantly more volume, or are you near capacity?
18. Have you lost any clients recently? If so, why did they leave?
19. What is your biggest operational challenge right now?

### 1D. Rate Structure & Quoting

20. Can you share your general rate ranges for common routes so I can have informed conversations with prospects?
21. How do you want me to handle rate inquiries — should I collect cargo details and pass to you, or will you give me a rate card?
22. What is your minimum shipment size — is there a point where a shipment is too small to be worth taking?
23. Do you offer credit terms to clients, or is it payment before shipment?

### 1E. Lead Handoff & Sales Process

24. When I find a potential client, what should I do next — introduce them to you directly, or gather their requirements first?
25. Do you want to handle all quotations yourself, or should I learn to prepare them?
26. How quickly can you turn around a quotation once you have cargo details?
27. Is there a commission or incentive structure for new clients I bring in? (Important to clarify early and in writing)
28. Who in your team should I coordinate with if you're not available?

## Part 2 — Building Your Lead List (Channels & Methods)

After your discovery meeting with Belal, use these channels to identify potential clients. Prioritize based on Belal's growth goals and your strongest personal connections.

---

### Channel 1 — Your Personal Network (Warm Leads — Start Here)

These are people who already know and trust you. Conversion is highest here.

- **Garment factory owners** you already know (you mentioned a couple — start with them)
- **AIBS connections** — Belal's friend / your business partner may know manufacturers and traders
- **Community and social contacts** — anyone in your Malaysian or Bangladeshi network who manufactures, imports, or exports physical goods
- **University / professional contacts** — former colleagues or classmates now in manufacturing or trading businesses

**Action:** Make a list of every person you know who either ships goods internationally or knows someone who does. Even indirect connections count — "I know someone who might know someone" is a valid starting point.

### Channel 2 — Industry Directories & Associations (Targeted Cold Leads)

These give you lists of companies that are already in international trade.

**Malaysia:**
- **FMM Directory** (Federation of Malaysian Manufacturers) — fmm.org.my — searchable database of Malaysian manufacturers by industry and location
- **MATRADE Exporter Directory** — matrade.gov.my — database of Malaysian exporters registered with Malaysia External Trade Development Corporation
- **SME Corp Directory** — smecorp.gov.my — database of Malaysian SMEs
- **Malaysia Yellow Pages / BusinessList.my** — filter by manufacturing, trading, import/export

**Bangladesh:**
- **BGMEA** (Bangladesh Garment Manufacturers and Exporters Association) — bgmea.com.bd — full member directory of garment exporters
- **BKMEA** (Bangladesh Knitwear Manufacturers and Exporters Association) — bkmea.com
- **FBCCI** (Federation of Bangladesh Chambers of Commerce and Industry) — fbcci.org
- **Bangladesh Export Promotion Bureau** — epb.gov.bd

**Action:** After your meeting with Belal, identify which industries and corridors he wants to grow in. Then search the relevant directories for companies matching that profile.

### Channel 3 — Online Platforms (Research & Outreach)

**LinkedIn:**
- Search for company pages of manufacturers and traders in Malaysia and Bangladesh
- Filter by industry, company size, and location
- Connect with logistics managers, supply chain heads, operations directors, and business owners
- Join freight forwarding and logistics groups for visibility

**Alibaba / Made-in-Malaysia / TradeIndia:**
- Malaysian and Bangladeshi manufacturers with active profiles on these platforms are already exporting — they use forwarders
- Their profiles often show what they export and to which countries — useful for qualifying leads

**Google Maps / Business Directories:**
- Search industrial areas near Belal's offices (Shah Alam, Klang, Seri Kembangan, Balakong) for manufacturing companies
- Many small manufacturers don't appear in official directories but are visible on Google Maps

### Channel 4 — Physical Proximity (Door-to-Door)

Industrial estates near Shah Alam and Klang are packed with manufacturers and warehouses. Many small to medium manufacturers don't actively look for forwarders online — they rely on whoever walks in or gets recommended.

**Key industrial areas to canvass:**
- Seksyen 15, Shah Alam (industrial zone)
- Kawasan Perindustrian Bukit Jelutong
- Klang industrial areas (near Port Klang — many import/export businesses)
- Balakong / Seri Kembangan industrial parks
- Hicom Glenmarie Industrial Park

**Action:** After a few weeks of working with Belal and understanding the business, visit these areas with business cards and a simple company profile. Introduce yourself, ask if they ship internationally, and leave your contact details.

### Channel 5 — Trigger Events (High-Intent Leads)

These are situations where a company suddenly needs a new forwarder or is entering international trade for the first time.

**Where to find trigger events:**
- MATRADE news and announcements — companies winning export contracts or entering new markets
- Trade shows and exhibitions (MIHAS for halal, FMM events, INTRADE Malaysia) — exhibitors are actively trading
- News articles about Malaysian or Bangladeshi companies expanding internationally
- Government export incentive programs — companies applying for these are planning to export

## Part 3 — Reaching Out (Value-First Approach)

### The Wrong Way
> *"Hi, I'm working with a freight forwarding company. Do you need freight forwarding services?"*

This is easy to say no to. It positions you as a salesperson and the prospect as a target.

### The Right Way — Lead with Curiosity and Value

**For warm leads (people you know):**
> *"Hey [name], I've recently started working with a freight forwarding company that handles global shipments. I'm curious — how are you currently handling your export shipments? Are you happy with the rates and service you're getting?"*

This opens a conversation. If they're happy, you learn about their current setup. If they're frustrated, you've found your opportunity.

**For cold leads (companies from directories or LinkedIn):**
> *"I noticed your company exports [product] to [region]. I work with a freight forwarding company with strong rates on that corridor. Would you be open to a quick comparison quote — no obligation — just to see if there's a cost saving?"*

The key phrase is **"comparison quote, no obligation."** You're not asking them to switch. You're offering them information. Low risk for them, high potential for you.

**For trigger event leads (new exporters, companies in transition):**
> *"Congratulations on the [new export contract / expansion to new market]. If you're looking for a freight partner for this, I'd be happy to connect you with our team for a quote. We specialize in [relevant corridor]."*

### What to Collect from Every Prospect

When a prospect shows interest, gather these details so Belal can prepare a quotation:

1. What product are they shipping? (Type, HS code if they know it)
2. Origin (pickup address or city)
3. Destination (delivery address or city/port)
4. Cargo quantity — number of cartons/pallets, dimensions, and weight
5. Frequency — one-time or regular shipments? How often?
6. Timeline — when does it need to arrive?
7. Current forwarder — who are they using now? (Helps Belal understand competition)
8. Pain points — any problems with their current setup? (Cost, delays, damage, communication)

## Part 4 — Recommended Tools

### CRM — Should You Use One?

**Not yet.** At your current stage — before your discovery meeting with Belal and with only a handful of potential leads — a full CRM is overkill. It will consume setup time without adding value.

**Start with a simple spreadsheet (Google Sheets).** Once you have 30+ leads and are actively managing conversations, then consider a CRM.

### Phase 1 Tools — Start With These Now

| Tool | Purpose | Cost |
|---|---|---|
| **Google Sheets** | Track leads — company name, contact person, phone/email, industry, corridor, status (new / contacted / quoted / won / lost), notes | Free |
| **WhatsApp Business** | Primary communication with Malaysian and Bangladeshi prospects — create a professional business profile with Belal's company name | Free |
| **LinkedIn (Free)** | Research companies, find decision-makers, connect and message | Free |
| **Google Alerts** | Set alerts for keywords like "Malaysian manufacturer export" or "Bangladesh garment new order" — trigger events delivered to your inbox | Free |
| **Canva** | Create a simple one-page company profile / flyer for Belal's business — useful for cold outreach and factory visits | Free tier available |

### Phase 2 Tools — Add These When You Have 30+ Active Leads

| Tool | Purpose | Cost |
|---|---|---|
| **HubSpot CRM (Free tier)** | Track leads, log calls and emails, set follow-up reminders, pipeline management — free for up to 1,000 contacts | Free |
| **Apollo.io or Hunter.io** | Find email addresses and contact details for companies — useful for cold outreach at scale | Free tiers available |
| **LinkedIn Sales Navigator** | Advanced search filters (company size, industry, geography), lead recommendations, InMail messaging | ~USD 80/month |

### Phase 3 Tools — Add These When Lead Generation Is Producing Revenue

| Tool | Purpose | Cost |
|---|---|---|
| **Zoho CRM or Salesforce Essentials** | Full CRM with automation, email sequences, reporting | USD 15–25/user/month |
| **Mailchimp or Brevo** | Email campaigns to prospects — newsletters, rate updates, service announcements | Free tiers available |
| **Calendly** | Let prospects book meetings with you or Belal directly | Free tier available |
