### The State of the Industry — Honest Assessment

**The good news first.**

Freight forwarding isn't going away. Physical goods will always need to move across borders. E-commerce is accelerating cross-border trade, not reducing it. Global trade volume continues to grow despite disruptions — every tariff war, every supply chain crisis, every pandemic ultimately reinforced that the world needs logistics professionals who can navigate complexity.

The global freight forwarding market is massive and growing. We're talking hundreds of billions of dollars. And here's what matters for someone like you — **the industry is heavily fragmented.** Unlike tech or banking, freight forwarding isn't dominated by five giant companies. There are thousands of small and medium forwarders globally, each serving their niches, corridors, and client relationships. Belal's company is one of them. There's room.

**Now the uncomfortable reality.**

The industry *is* changing, and the changes are real:

**Digital freight platforms** — companies like Flexport, Freightos, and Zencargo are doing to traditional forwarders what online travel agencies did to travel agents. A shipper can now get instant quotes, book shipments, and track cargo online without calling anyone. The "I know the rates and you don't" advantage that traditional forwarders rely on is eroding.

**AI and automation** — document processing, customs classification, rate comparison, shipment tracking, even quotation generation — all of these are being automated. The routine tasks that I identified as your entry point (quotations, tracking, documentation follow-up) are exactly the tasks most vulnerable to automation over the next 5–10 years.

**Margin compression** — as pricing becomes more transparent through digital platforms, the margin a forwarder can charge on carrier rates is shrinking. The days of making USD 250 markup on a container by simply having access to rates are declining.

**So what survives?**

The forwarders who survive and thrive are the ones who provide **complex problem-solving, relationship-based service, and specialized expertise** — not the ones who just pass along rates and track containers. Specifically, the value shifts toward handling complicated cargo (dangerous goods, oversized, temperature-sensitive), navigating regulatory complexity (sanctions, trade compliance, halal logistics), managing crisis situations (port strikes, vessel delays, customs holds), and deep specialization in specific corridors or industries.
